Si Tanggang

A poor widow raised her son, Si Tanggang, with great love and sacrifice. Despite their poverty, she gave him everything she could. When Tanggang grew up, he left to seek his fortune at sea and eventually became a wealthy merchant.

Years later, his grand ship returned to his home village. His elderly mother, recognizing her son, approached the vessel with joy. But Tanggang, now dressed in fine clothes and surrounded by his wealthy wife and crew, denied knowing this poor, ragged woman. He was ashamed of her poverty and refused to acknowledge her as his mother.

Heartbroken and humiliated, his mother raised her hands to the sky and cursed him: "If you are truly my son who has forgotten his mother, may you and your ship turn to stone!"

Instantly, Tanggang, his ship, and his crew were transformed into stone, frozen forever as punishment for his ingratitude and shame.

Moral: Never forget those who sacrificed for you. Ingratitude and shame towards one's parents brings terrible consequences.

Batu Belah Batu Bertangkup

A widowed mother worked tirelessly to care for her two children, but they were ungrateful and neglectful. One day, she prepared a special dish using fish eggs (ikan tembakul) that she had been craving. While she was away, her children returned home hungry and carelessly ate all the food, including their mother's portion of the prized fish eggs.

When the mother returned and discovered what they had done, her heart brokeâ€”not from hunger, but from their thoughtlessness and lack of care for her. Overcome with grief and anger, she wandered into the forest.

The children, realizing their mistake too late, rushed after their mother, calling and begging her to return. But she continued forward, and the rock swallowed her completely before closing shut forever, leaving the children orphaned and filled with regret.

Moral: Take care of those who sacrifice for you. Negligence and taking parents for granted can lead to irreversible loss.

Bawang Putih Bawang Merah

After Bawang Putih's kind mother died, her father remarried a cruel woman who brought her own daughter, Bawang Merah. When her father also passed away, Bawang Putih was treated as a servant, forced to do all the housework while her stepsister was pampered.

One day, while washing clothes by the river, Bawang Putih met a beautiful golden fish. The fish spoke to her with a familiar, loving voiceâ€”it was her mother's spirit, reincarnated to watch over her. Every day, Bawang Putih would visit the fish, sharing her sorrows, and the fish would comfort her and give her advice.

The stepmother discovered this secret friendship and became jealous of any kindness shown to Bawang Putih. One day, she caught the golden fish, killed it, and cooked it into a meal. Worst of all, she forced Bawang Putih to eat it, not telling her what it was until after she had finished.

Devastated and heartbroken, Bawang Putih wept by the river. But her mother's spirit appeared to her in a dream, telling her to bury the fish bones in the garden. When Bawang Putih did so, a magical tree grew overnight, bearing golden fruit and beautiful clothes.

A prince passing by saw Bawang Putih in the fine clothes from the tree and fell in love with her kindness and beauty. They married, and Bawang Putih finally found happiness. Meanwhile, the stepmother and Bawang Merah, consumed by jealousy and greed, tried to replicate her fortune but failed, remaining bitter and poor for the rest of their lives.

Moral: A mother's love transcends even death. Kindness and patience will be rewarded, while cruelty only brings emptiness. Evil cannot destroy goodness permanently.
